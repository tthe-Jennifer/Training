Here is a timeline of Mr. Achebe's life:

  • 1930 - Chinua Achebe was born to his Nigerian parents in Ogidi, British Nigeria
  • 1948 - Started studying at the university of Ibadan, Nigeria
  • 1953-1956 - Started teaching and encouraged his students to be original in their work
  • 1957 - 2012 - Published a variety of accredited novels poems and short stories, Including highly praised Things fall apart" and his last publication There Was a Country: A Personal History of Biafra
  • 1972-1975 - Professorship in two American universities
  • 1975 - heavily critized Joseph Conrad's - The heart of darkness for its racist and prejucide content against people of African heritage
  • 2000-2012 - Won several awards including - The Dorothy and Lillian Gish Prize
  • 2013 - Dies after a brief illness in Boston, US.
As Achebe said, “If you don’t like someone’s story, you tell your own.” And Africans should not wait for tributes to their heroes to be told by others, as encapsulated in Achebe’s memorable proverb:“If nobody will praise me, I will praise myself, said the Lizard which fell from the high Iroko tree.” Many would have witnessed that the lizard, on falling, nods its head repeatedly in appreciation of its feat.
